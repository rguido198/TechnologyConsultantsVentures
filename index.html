02

Where it pays

Three industries. One question: does it pay for itself?

Not client case studies: realistic examples of where the automation and agent patterns above turn into measurable ROI, based on problems we hear about often in these industries.

Restaurant &
multi-location food service

Illustrative Scenario

An ordering agent that closes the loop — and knows when to hand off.

A conversational agent takes the order by phone, drive-thru mic, or chat, confirms it against the live menu and today’s inventory, and sends it straight to the kitchen display and payment system. The design isn’t “the AI handles everything”, it’s a deterministic escalation path: an allergen question, an unusual substitution, or a frustrated customer routes to a person immediately.

The math ↳

The AI drive-thru failures that made headlines tried to handle every case themselves. Automating the routine orders and escalating the rest keeps the labor savings real — without ever shipping an order nobody asked for.

Commercial
real estate

Illustrative Scenario

Turning weeks of lease abstraction into a same-day review.

Every lease, amendment, and estoppel sits in a PDF someone has to read line by line to find the rent escalation, the renewal window, the CAM reconciliation clause. An agent-based abstraction pipeline extracts key terms into structured data, flags anything ambiguous for a human reviewer, and surfaces upcoming deadlines before they're missed.

The math ↳

A missed renewal-notice date can cost more in lost leverage than a year of the system that would have caught it.

Investment
management

Illustrative Scenario

Getting through the filing before the market moves on it.

Analysts spend hours pulling the same handful of numbers out of 10-Ks, earnings transcripts, and portfolio reports before the actual analysis can start. A validation-first extraction pipeline pulls the structured metrics, checks them against source, and hands the analyst a reviewed summary instead of a stack of PDFs.

The math ↳

Hours reclaimed per filing add up across a portfolio faster than the system costs to run — and deterministic validation means no one's trusting a hallucinated number in a memo.

These scenarios are illustrative: built from the same patterns above, not completed client engagements.

Smash Bacon · Muelle 8 Seafood — Live · Square + Voice AI

A phone line that stops missing orders, and a checkout that skips the delivery-app cut.

The problem: two National City restaurants under one owner, a burger spot and a seafood counter, were losing orders to missed calls during rushes and giving up 20 to 30 percent of every delivery-app order in commission. The system: complete rebuilds of both sites, structured to be found and read correctly by AI search and shopping agents, not just Google.

The order layer: both sites connect directly to Square for pickup orders, so a customer can order and pay without a third-party app in between. The phone layer: a Voice AI line now answers the calls the team couldn't get to, takes the order, and pushes it straight into the POS.

Appt Helper · Open Cita — Live · MCP + x402

An agent-native alert engine, catching government slots that vanish in minutes.

The problem: Global Entry & SENTRI cancellations disappear within minutes, and no official alert exists. The system: an automated pipeline watches all 184 U.S. enrollment centers 24/7, detects an opening the moment it appears, and fires an SMS seconds later — cutting a 4-to-11-month wait to days.

The agent layer: it exposes an MCP server so AI agents can query live availability as a tool, and accepts autonomous, pay-per-use payments over Coinbase’s x402 protocol. That's agent-native infrastructure, not just a website.

TypeScript · Next.js · Postgres · Twilio · MCP · x402 (Coinbase)

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is your typical engagement model?

We operate either on a scoped, milestone-driven project basis (MVPs, redesigns, specific integrations) or as a fractional CTO retainer for ongoing senior development and operational capacity.

Do you advertise pricing?

Projects are tailored to each client's specific business context and technical complexity. We scope every project individually to provide transparent, flat-rate phase pricing rather than pre-advertised rates.

What is your standard technology stack?

React and TypeScript on the front-end, Node.js on the back-end, and PostgreSQL for databases. Our AI work leverages Claude, OpenAI, and custom Model Context Protocol (MCP) integrations.

How is technical handoff managed?

We deliver fully documented git repositories and assist with cloud deployment. Your internal team can take over immediately, or we can continue running and monitoring the setup under a retainer.

Where are you based?

San Diego, California, working with clients globally across the United States and Mexican cross-border markets.
